Project Overview

BeeMath is a math crossword puzzle application designed to enhance math skills, problem solving abilities, and critical thinking for children aged 8–12 years. Developed on Android mobile platform using Unity. This project was created as part of a Project Based Learning (PjBL) program, where I contributed in the areas of UI design, asset creation, and music production.

Project Background

In today’s digital era, elementary school children frequently access online games. Indonesia is recorded as having the third-largest online gaming market in the world, yet much of its content lacks educational value.

Many students struggle to grasp basic mathematical concepts because they find math to be a boring subject. Additionally, the math proficiency of Indonesian students declined in 2022.

Solution

To address this challenge, we designed BeeMath with the following features:

  • Tiered-level educational puzzles
  • A mascot character, engaging visuals, and child-friendly design
  • A scoring system and leaderboard

User Target

Targeted at children aged 8–12, in line with the stage of learning basic arithmetic operations, the crossmath game offers a fun and interactive learning experience. Additionally, BeeMath is also suitable for users over the age of 12 who want to sharpen their calculation skills while enjoying a challenge.

Objectives

  • Helping users improve their math skills
  • Helping users develop problem-solving and critical thinking abilities
  • Providing an entertaining yet challenging medium for learning

Benefits

  • Enhancing math skills
  • Sharpening problem-solving and critical thinking abilities
  • Learning through play

Process

Semester 1: Initial Exploration & Visual Design

The main focus for the first semester was to establish the visual and functional foundation of this educational math game. We adopted a Design Thinking approach, but had not yet reached the stage of direct user testing. Due to time and technical constraints, user testing was not conducted in this phase.

Empathize

As a team, we discussed the needs of children aged 8 to 12. I helped identify the core learning challenges in math.

Define

Together, we defined our goal: helping children learn math through a fun, bee-themed crossword game.

Ideate

I contributed to developing the game concept with a cheerful visual tone, featuring Bima the bee as our main guide.

Prototype

I designed the initial UI in Figma, using simple illustrations and a minimal gameplay layout.

Coding

The coding phase was handled by the developers in our team. We faced some technical issues with the leaderboard feature.

Improvements and Development

After discussion, we decided to carry out several improvements and developments, including:

  • Fixing bugs in the game.
  • Improving the question system in the game.
  • Adding a storyboard.
  • Enhancing the gameplay appearance, such as by adding a background.

Note

Due to time constraints and the technical limitations of the team, the application still has a number of unresolved bugs. Some features, such as the leaderboard, the level-locking system on the game map, and hint feature, are not yet functioning optimally. Nevertheless, this process has provided valuable lessons, especially in terms of planning, collaboration, and applying user feedback for future development.
