Catto: Learn to Read, Write, and Count While Playing

Project Based Learning (PjBL) – SMK Negeri 8 Semarang (Jul 2023 - Jun 2024)

My Role

  • UI Designer
  • Game Asset Designer

Tools

  • Figma
  • Adobe Illustrator

Project Overview

Catto is a reading, writing, and arithmetic learning app for children aged 3-9. Children learn while playing in an imaginative world called Catto's World. They are guided by fictional characters who develop their imaginations.

Project Background

  • Internet usage is increasing rapidly: In the past five years, household internet usage has reached 78.18%, which has also influenced family cultural patterns.
  • The impact of technology on children: Children are now playing outside less often due to the influence of technology.
  • Reading literacy issues: A case in Cianjur revealed that a fourth-grade student could not yet read, as revealed by direct testing.
  • National phenomenon: Many children in Indonesia still have difficulty reading, influenced by various factors, one of which is a lack of student engagement in learning.

Solution

To address this challenge, we designed Catto with the following features:

  • Create a learning app for reading, writing, and arithmetic that prioritizes children's active participation.
  • The learning is designed around mini-games to prevent children from getting bored quickly, and there are time limits on app usage to prevent children from playing too much.
  • After completing the learning, children will receive rewards and receive results, which will be shared in parent mode.

User Target

The target users of this read, write, and count application are children aged 3-9 years who need to learn to read, write, and count in a fun way, as well as parents who accompany and monitor their children's learning development through the application.

Objectives

  • Leveraging technology to support efficient education.
  • Changing the stigma of learning that children find boring and confusing.
  • Improving children's cognitive function through play and making learning enjoyable.

Benefits

  • Helping children become more independent, confident, and ready for elementary school.
  • It fosters communication, curiosity, and the ability to adapt to new environments.
  • Makes learning fun through mini-games, while also being a safe and educational mobile game option.
